Acupressure and Vomiting of Pregnancy

Nausea and vomiting are problems most women encounter during pregnancy. Symptoms usually last for 8 to 12 weeks. Nausea and vomiting have a great impact on pregnant women, including psychological and physical.

According to traditional Chinese medicine, pregnancy vomiting is due to the accumulation of qi and blood in the uterus to nourish the fetus in the early stage of pregnancy, but the fetus in the early stage of pregnancy is smaller and needs less qi and blood, which leads to the loss of balance of stomach qi and vomiting. When pregnancy reached three months, the amount of qi and blood needed by the fetus began to increase, the whole body qi and blood began to stabilize, and the reaction of pregnancy and vomiting gradually disappeared.

Most pregnant women are sceptical about the safety of using drugs to relieve nausea and vomiting during pregnancy. Acupoint stimulation is a commonly used treatment in traditional Chinese medicine, its effectiveness has long been confirmed by clinical research, although the mechanism of action is not clear.

Acupoint massage is gradually welcomed because of its effectiveness, safety, and physical and psychological comfort.

Naeimi Rad, M. et al. Note that pressing KID21 Point (Youmen) could relieve nausea and vomiting during pregnancy. The data showed that the frequency of nausea (P < 0.001, U ≥ 228.5) and vomiting (P < 0.001, U ≥ 380) were different between the groups on the fourth day of the study.

The results showed that the effect of pressing KID21 group was better than that of the control group in the first three months of treatment of vomiting during pregnancy. And no side effects were found.
